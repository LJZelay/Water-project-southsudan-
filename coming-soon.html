INTRODUCTION

Welcome Scholar!

▼ EXPAND

You are entering a world that was never meant to be controlled. A place where water decides the rhythm of life, where land appears and disappears with the seasons, and where entire civilizations move in sync with a living ecosystem. But something is coming... a force that sees this world not as life, but as "waste." A line will be drawn across the wetlands. A system will be broken. And the balance that held everything together will begin to collapse. In this experience, you will witness the rise of the Jonglei Canal, not as a simple project, but as a collision between power and survival, engineering and ecology, control and resistance. The question is not just what happened… but what was lost and what is still at stake.

HOW TO NAVIGATE THE EXPERIENCE

Get the Best Experience!

▼ EXPAND
  • Take your time :this isn't meant to be rushed. Pause, read, listen, and look closely at details in each scene.
  • Click on everything interactive : characters, objects, and visuals all reveal hidden layers (dialogue, perspectives, facts).
  • Listen to the voices : when characters speak, pay attention to their concerns, emotions, and experiences. This is where the human impact becomes real.
  • Follow the transitions between scenes : notice how the environment changes over time (before, during, and after the canal). That shift is the story.
  • Engage with quotes and protest elements : they represent real resistance, not just background visuals.
  • Look for patterns : flooding, disease, movement, conflict. These are not random, they connect across the entire experience.
  • Spend time in the "present-day" section : this is where everything comes together. This is not just history , it's still ongoing.
  • Reflect as you go : ask yourself, Who benefits? Who is harmed? What was ignored?
  • Use the experience, don't just view it : this is not just a website, it's something you explore, question, and piece together yourself.
  • Use your tools to enagage! Use your mouse/mousepad to hold and drag the camera of the scenes to look around and zoom in with mouse to interact with the environement.
  • If you are in a rush (looking at you graders), no worries! Feel free to click on any scene below to jump directly there. I knew this might be useful!
